# 🚆 Metro Electrification Logistics Plan

## 1. Project Overview
**Scope:**  
Supply, transportation, storage, and distribution of all materials, equipment, and consumables required for:
- Overhead Equipment (OHE) System  
- Traction Substations (TSS) and Auxiliary Substations (ASS)  
- SCADA and Power Supply Cabling  
- Third Rail
- Earthing, bonding, and related civil interface works  

**Objective:**  
Ensure uninterrupted material and equipment supply to all workfronts in alignment with the project schedule and safety standards.

---

## 2. Logistics Strategy

| Aspect | Strategy |
|--------|-----------|
| **Material Flow** | Source → Central Store → Field Store → Site |
| **Storage** | Covered warehouse for electrical items; open yard for steel & OHE masts |
| **Transportation** | Mix of containerized, flatbed, and heavy-duty trailers based on material type |
| **Control System** | ERP-based Material Tracking (GRN, Issue, Consumption) |
| **Safety** | Mandatory PPE, lifting plans, route clearance for heavy transport |

---

## 3. Material Classification

| Category | Examples | Storage Type |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **OHE Components** | Masts, Portals, Brackets, Insulators, Catenary Wire, Contact Wire | Open Yard / Covered |
| **TSS Equipment** | Transformers, Switchgear, Circuit Breakers, Panels | Covered Warehouse |
| **Cables** | Power & Control Cables, SCADA Cables | Drum Storage on Cable Racks |
| **Hardware & Fasteners** | Bolts, Nuts, Clamps | Shelving System |
| **Consumables** | Earthing rods, Welding rods, Lugs | Controlled Environment |
| **Tools & Plants** | Tower Wagons, Cranes, MEWPs, EOT Cranes | Equipment Yard |

---

## 4. Transportation Plan

| Activity | Responsibility | Remarks |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Vendor dispatch coordination | Vendor + Logistics Manager | Dispatch as per call-off plan |
| Route survey for oversized loads | Logistics Manager | Especially for TSS transformers |
| Material tracking | Logistics Coordinator | GPS/ERP tracking |
| Unloading arrangements | Site Logistics Team | Cranes, forklifts, slings as per load plan |
| Inspection after delivery | QC + Logistics | Prepare Material Receipt Report |

---


## 5 Cost Control Measures

- Optimize delivery batching to minimize freight cost.  
- Consolidate shipments from nearby suppliers.  
- Use reverse logistics for return of reusable packing materials.  
- Maintain cost sheet for each delivery activity (fuel, freight, handling, demurrage).

---

## 6. Risk & Mitigation Plan

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|
| Delay in material delivery | Buffer stock + Alternate suppliers |
| Damage during transport | Proper packaging + Insurance |
| Congested site access | Delivery scheduling at off-peak hours |
| Theft/pilferage | Fencing + Security guards + CCTV |
| Weather exposure | Covered storage + tarpaulin covers |

---

## 7. Reporting & Review

- **Daily:** Material receipt and issue report  
- **Weekly:** Delivery status vs. plan  
- **Monthly:** Material reconciliation and cost summary  

**KPI Tracking:**
- % On-time Deliveries  
- % Material Availability at Site  
- Logistics Cost vs. Budget

# 🚆 Metro Electrification Project – Logistics & Procurement Flow

## 1. Objective
To define a structured workflow for managing the end-to-end **Logistics and Procurement process** for metro electrification works (OHE, TSS, SCADA, and associated systems).  
This ensures all materials and equipment are procured, inspected, transported, and delivered in full compliance with **technical specifications, engineering requirements, and contractual timelines.**

---

## 2. Process Participants & Responsibilities (RACI Matrix)

| Function | Key Responsibility | Role |
|-----------|--------------------|------|
| **Design Engineer (Contractor/Engineering)** | Provides specifications, datasheets, and technical review | Accountable |
| **Logistics Manager** | Prepares RFQs, coordinates quotations, manages technical numbering (DOORS), prepares financial comparison & final PO | Responsible |
| **Procurement Officer / CPM** | Approves vendor selection and PO placement | Accountable |
| **Quality / QA-QC Team** | Conducts inspection and testing coordination | Support |
| **Project Control / Planning** | Tracks progress, delivery schedule, and updates reports | Support |
| **Vendor / OEM** | Supplies material as per approved specs and delivery schedule | Responsible |

---

## 3. End-to-End Logistics & Procurement Flow

### **Step 1: Engineering Inputs**
- Design Engineer provides:
  - Confirmed **Specifications & Datasheets**
  - **Type Test Reports** (for reference)
  - **Transportation & Handling Requirements**
  - **Bill of Quantities (BOQ)**
- Each line item is assigned a **DOORS Number** (Design Output Objective Requirement Sheet) for technical tracking.
- Engineering may refer to **approved vendor lists (AVLs)**, but sourcing flexibility remains with Logistics.

---

### **Step 2: RFQ (Request for Quotation) Preparation**
- Logistics prepares and issues **RFQs** to multiple OEMs/manufacturers ensuring:
  - Compliance with **technical, type test, and quality requirements**
  - Inclusion of:
    - Scope of supply
    - GA drawings
    - Packing, handling, and delivery conditions
    - Warranty & service obligations
  - Reference to corresponding **DOORS Numbers**
- Logistics collects all vendor quotations for review.

---

### **Step 3: Technical Evaluation (Engineering Review)**
- Engineering team reviews vendor offers for:
  - Compliance with specifications, datasheets, and BOQ.
  - Alignment with type test and design requirements.
- **Engineering prepares the Technical Comparison Sheet (TCS)**.
- Non-compliance queries are resolved between Engineering and vendors through Logistics coordination.
- Once technically acceptable, **Engineering issues clearance for procurement (ECP)**.

---

### **Step 4: Commercial Evaluation**
- Logistics prepares **Financial Comparison Sheets (FCS)** for each technically cleared item/vendor.
- Financial comparison includes:
  - Base price
  - Taxes & duties
  - Freight, insurance, and unloading costs
  - Payment terms
- Engineering-approved and **commercially lowest (L1)** vendor is identified for procurement.

---

### **Step 5: Purchase Order (PO) Placement**
- Logistics issues **Purchase Order (PO)** after:
  - Approval from **Project Manager and CPM**
  - Verification of compliance with technical and commercial conditions
- PO includes:
  - Detailed technical references (specs, datasheets, DOORS nos.)
  - Packing and transportation requirements
  - Inspection and QA/QC protocols
  - Delivery schedule and reporting format
- Copy of PO shared with Engineering, QA/QC, and Planning for follow-up.

---

### **Step 6: Inspection & Dispatch Clearance**
- QA/QC coordinates with OEM for:
  - **Pre-Dispatch Inspection (PDI)**
  - **Factory Acceptance Test (FAT)** (if required)
- Inspection reports are submitted to Logistics before dispatch clearance.
- Only inspected and approved materials are authorized for shipment.

---

### **Step 7: Transportation & Delivery Logistics**
- Logistics arranges:
  - **Transportation mode** (trailer, container, or heavy-duty)
  - **Route clearance** for oversized cargo
  - **Insurance** for in-transit materials
  - **Unloading equipment** (crane, forklift) at destination
- Delivery coordinated with site team as per workfront requirement.
- **Material Receipt Report (MRR)** prepared at site upon delivery.

---

### **Step 8: Documentation & Record Control**
All transactions are tracked **manually** (Excel-based) with DOORS linkage.

**Key Records:**
- RFQ Register  
- Vendor Response Log  
- Technical Comparison Sheet (Engineering)  
- Financial Comparison Sheet (Logistics)  
- Purchase Order Tracker  
- Delivery & Inspection Reports  
- DOORS Tracking Sheet (for closure)

---

### **Step 9: Review & Coordination**
- **Weekly coordination meetings** held between:
  - Engineering
  - Logistics
  - Procurement
  - QA/QC
  - Planning
- Discuss and resolve:
  - Pending RFQs and clarifications
  - Technical approvals and ECPs
  - PO placements and dispatch plans
  - Material delivery status
- Updates reflected in **DOORS Closure Register** and **Procurement Dashboard.**

---

### **Step 10: Post-Delivery & Site Handover**
- Logistics ensures:
  - Material unloading at designated storage area
  - Verification against PO & Delivery Challan
  - Safe handling and storage
- Any damage or short supply reported immediately.
- Final reconciliation shared with Planning and Engineering for project documentation.

## 5. Key Control Points
1. All items must have **unique DOORS references** for tracking.  
2. **Engineering clearance** mandatory before commercial evaluation.  
3. **PDI/FAT clearance** required before dispatch.  
4. **Insurance and inspection certificates** to accompany each shipment.  
5. **Weekly progress updates** mandatory for management review.
